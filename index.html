What this dashboard does

This tool helps you plan how many leads, meetings, orders, and installations you can expect next month — using patterns from the last few months of real data, not guesswork. You can look at the whole company, one city, one referral channel, or a specific combination of both. And wherever the automatic plan doesn't feel right, you can adjust it by hand — nothing here is locked.

The 5 numbers we track

Every lead moves through five stages, always in this order:

→

HOTO

Between each stage we track a percentage — how many people at one stage make it to the next. We call these the funnel rates. For example, if 100 leads come in and 60 of them get a meeting booked, that's a 60% BQL→MS rate.

How we guess next month's numbers

Nothing here is pulled out of thin air. This is the actual process, every time:

  1. Look back at your last few complete months (3 by default — you can change this in Settings).
  2. Work out the average level, and whether things have been trending up or down.
  3. Stretch that trend forward to the month you're planning for. Planning 2 months ahead instead of 1 stretches it twice as far.
  4. Do this separately for the number of leads (BQL) and for each of the four percentages, then multiply everything through in order to get MS, MD, Order, and HOTO.

If the month you're sitting in right now is still in progress (not finished yet), we estimate what it'll probably add up to by comparing it to how earlier months looked at the same point in time — not just by assuming an even, flat pace across the whole month.

How to change a number

Click on any number or percentage, type a new value, and press Enter. It turns orange to show you've changed it by hand. To undo it, click it again, clear the box, and press Enter — it goes back to the automatic estimate.

There are two different kinds of things you can change, and they behave differently — this is the single most important thing to understand about this tool:

If you change a plain number (BQL, meetings, orders, installs...): the percentages stay exactly as they were, and every other number recalculates to stay consistent with those percentages — both the ones that come before it and the ones that come after it.

Example: you think Installs (HOTO) will actually be higher than shown. Type in the new number. Your percentages don't move — instead the tool works backward using those same percentages: recalculating Orders, then Meetings Done, then Meetings Booked, then Leads, so everything still fits together.

If you change a percentage: only what comes after that percentage changes. Everything before it stays exactly the same.

Example: improve the "Meeting Booked → Meeting Done" percentage, and Meetings Done, Orders, and Installs all update. But Leads and Meetings Booked themselves don't move, because they come before that step in the chain.

Where changes travel between pages

Where you make a change matters — some changes stay local, others spread out to everywhere else.

One more thing worth knowing: if you've changed a City's number by hand, it always wins — even if a Sub-Channel change would normally spread into it, your city number stays exactly as you set it.

Fixing the current month

The month you're currently in usually isn't finished yet, so the dashboard makes its best guess for how it'll turn out — based on how many days have passed, compared to how earlier months looked at the same point.

If you know something the automatic guess doesn't (say, a specific city had an unusually good or bad week), go to the Current Month tab and correct it there. That correction is used everywhere this month's data matters for future planning, not just in that one tab. Once the month is completely finished and the real numbers are all in, your correction simply stops being needed — the real numbers take over on their own.

Initiatives

An initiative is your way of saying "we're planning something that should change the numbers" — a marketing push, a bigger sales team, a process improvement — separate from the everyday trend.

When you add one, you choose:

  • Who it affects — everyone, one city, or one channel
  • What it changes — a number (like Orders) or a percentage (like Meeting Booked → Meeting Done)
  • How much — either a percentage increase, or a flat amount

The dashboard folds that into the plan automatically, on top of the regular trend.

Common questions

Why did the numbers change when I only changed my Planning Month?
Because how far ahead you're planning changes how far the trend gets stretched forward. Planning 2 months out applies the trend twice; 1 month out applies it once.

Why does a small city show a strange 100% or 0%?
Small cities have very few leads, so one or two people moving through the funnel can swing the percentage a lot. It's not a mistake — it's just what happens with small numbers. Check the Historical tab to see the real figures behind it.

I changed something and other numbers I didn't touch moved too — is that a bug?
No — that's the tool keeping everything consistent. See "How to change a number" and "Where changes travel" above.

How do I completely undo a change?
